## Tutorial Overview

This tutorial is designed to thoroughly introduce the basic concepts, advantages, and quick-start guidance for the **Undertale Changer Template** (hereafter referred to as UCT).

**UCT** is a fan game development template for **Undertale**, built on the **Unity engine**, using the **Universal Render Pipeline (URP)**.  
It is tailored for **full-scale development** or **stylized game development**, and designed to meet advanced requirements such as **full gameplay workflow**, **3D visuals**, and **Live2D** integration.

Key features include:

- **Built on the Unity engine**
- **Support for 2D lighting and 3D models**
- **Fully open source** (under the **GNU GPLv3** license, see [**License, Monetization, and Copyright Notice**](https://github.com/Bli-AIk/Undertale-Changer-Template/blob/main/LICENSE_MONETIZATION_AND_COPYRIGHT_NOTICE_zh-CN.md))
- **Community extensibility support**
- **Complete localization support**

> [!WARNING] âš ï¸ A word of caution...
> If you only need to develop **a single battle or a simple scene**, UCT may not be the best fit.  
> In that case, it might be more appropriate to develop using projects like [**Undertale Engine**](https://github.com/TML233/UndertaleEngine) or [**Create Your Frisk**](https://github.com/RhenaudTheLukark/CreateYourFrisk).

But if you're aiming to break out of conventional frameworks and want to experience **full-scale** or **highly stylized development**, UCT will no doubt offer a more compelling solution.
